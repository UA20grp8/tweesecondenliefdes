Design disciplines

In this section, we introduce you to the design disciplines that have left an inspiring impression on us. The following projects were placed from less to most inspiring.

Femme Atelier

DOORFRAME AS FURNITURE

The furniture was designed by the Bangkok studio 'Femme atelier. The furniture was made from recycled door and window frames. These constructions were then later filled in with colored glass.

What do we find interesting about 'Doorframe as furniture'?

Reuse of materials that one would not think of at first. The beautiful pastel colors give the project an extra touch.

What is multidisciplinary about 'Doorframe as furniture' here?

Use of windows and doors that are recycled for furniture design. 

THE BICYCLE

An object that will last for many years in our society. It is used for various purposes, one being used as a means of transport for home-work traffic. Another uses it as a relaxation agent.

What do we find interesting about the 'bicycle'?

Inspiring design because it has been around for a long time without necessarily having to change, fits in very well with human nature, gives a feeling of freedom and connection with nature. Good example of sustainable design, also interesting that cycling and walking have become our only ways of freedom in a certain way. 

What is multidisciplinary about the 'bicycle'?

Touches many areas within the design itself (use of materials, design ...). 

Walter Van Beirendonck and Dirk Van Saene

THE PAPER DRESS

Two well-known Antwerp designers designed a paper dress in collaboration with the mail order company La Redoute in 2009. In theory, the dress could be worn unscathed, because of the use of polyester with woven paper. 250 units of this design were made and sold. 

What do we find interesting about the 'Paper dress'?

It is not immediately apparent that the dress is made of paper. One would think it is a thick satin fabric. It creates the idea of ​​being functional, but once you start using it, it can also quickly break down. New way of looking at materials. 

What is multidisciplinary about the 'Paper dress'?

Use of paper in fashion industry.

Maarten Baas

REAL TIMES

This clock is located at Schiphol Airport. The clock is characterized by its performance. Every hand is painted. For example, you can see someone wiping the hands away for 24 hours and putting them back as if there is a man in the clock. 

What do we find interesting about 'Real times'?

This is a performance art instead of a static work. It attracts attention in its environment and people pause for a moment. 

What is multidisciplinary about 'Real times'?

The designer steps outside his own field of work. 

Iris van Herpen

CATHEDRAL DRESS

In this design, gothic architecture, woodworking and fashion come together in a design. The garment is extremely detailed and refined, which amazes the design time after time again. All parts can be easily distinguished from each other and have been carefully brought together in a playful design. If you look at the functionality, you will not easily see someone walking around with this object.

What do we find interesting about the 'Cathedral dress'?

The material is applied in a completely different way than usual. 

What is multidisciplinary about the 'Cathedral dress'?

Using an architectural style in fashion. 

Andrés Reisinger and Julia Esqué

HORTENSIA CHAIR

Andrés Reisinger made a CGI (digital image) of a chair covered with rose petals. After this image went viral on Instagram, he decided to check whether he could actually make the chair. Everyone he spoke to told him it was impossible to make the petals move in such a way as in his digital drawings. Until he found Julia Esqué, a product designer specialized in textile development. Together they have been working on the project for a year to make the fabric blow in the wind just like real flower petals.

What do we find interesting about the 'Hortensia chair'?

Applicable in a certain sense in today's society. People would like to get off the digital life and return back to a more physical one. 

What is multidisciplinary about the 'Hortensia chair'?

Total transfer from one discipline to another. People go from a digital image to a furniture design.

Maarten Baas

TREE TRUNK CHAIR

The Tree Trunk Chair is a chair designed by Maarten Baas and cannot be used yet. It is a future-oriented project that a few generations after us will be able to use. He designed a mold from which a functional chair can be created and places it next to a young planted tree. Throughout the years the tree will grow and become one with the mold. If the process goes according to the plan, this chair could be removed from the tree in 200 years' time.

What do we find interesting about 'The Tree Trunk chair'?

It is a very functional design, but on the other hand, one has to exercise a lot of patience to ever use it. The success rate of this project is influenced by its environment. When the tree gets sick or falls over, the design will fail.

What is multidisciplinary about 'The Tree Trunk chair'?

Recycle lamp elements in art. 

Installations

In this part we introduce you to art installations that have left an inspiring impression on us. The following installations were placed from less to most inspiring.

Klaus Pinter’s

REBOUNDS

This installation by Klaus Pinter is very astonishing at first. The inflated plastic balls influence the perception and experience of the old Romanesque space. It creates alienation because the gigantic balls partly float. The work thus responds to the feeling of the materials, their lightness, their loud nature and mechanical aspect. In this work, art, history and culture come together in an extravagant installation. The reflection makes the building look what looks like an MC Esher drawing. The installation's placement is based on the ideology of Plato who speculated about the nature of the physical world and humans. 

What do we find interesting about 'Rebounds'?

The impact of the artwork on its environment, but also vice versa, the impact of the environment on the artwork, is a very interesting fact.

What is multidisciplinary about 'Rebounds'?

Processing a philosophical idea into a work of art. 

Jean Dubuffet

JARDIN D’EMAIL

A black and white landscape that forms a playground for children but still remains a work of art.

What do we find interesting about 'Jardin D'email'?

People are part of the work. Several functions come together.

What is multidisciplinary about 'Jardin D'email'?

Merging public space and art.

Mareo Rodriguez

M.E.L.T.S.

In his exhibition series 'MELTS', which stands for 'matter, energy, light, time, space', Rodriguez shows some tangible installations by dealing with relief and light in an interesting way. With this he portrays topography as a living energy that is constantly in motion. The philosophy behind his work is therefore 'matter is condensed light'. 

What do we find interesting about 'M.E.L.T.S.'?

Topography is a completely different discipline, very interesting that inspiration was drawn from it.

What is multidisciplinary about 'M.E.L.T.S.'?

Processing the topography in art.

Brian Jungen

WARRIOR

Brian Jungen created a collection of installations made from old Nike Air Jordan Shoes. With this he made various variations on the native hairstyles. This is where the name Warrior comes from.

What do we find interesting about 'Warrior'?

New uses attributed to materials such as shoes here. It can be interesting to convert this into a contemporary problem, eg mouth masks. It reminds us of the duality of corona. On the one hand, it is good for the environment, for instance (less traffic, more rest for nature), but on the other hand, all masks do cause more pollution on the street. 

What is multidisciplinary about 'Warrior'?

Use of materials from the fashion world in art.

Caitlind R.C. Brown & Wayne Garrett

CLOUD

This installation contains no less than 6000 lights, of which ± 5000 were donated by the public itself. Each lamp has its own pull string to ignite the lamp. The use of pulling ropes in the coastal work creates interactions between two strange individuals. 

What do we find interesting about 'Cloud'?

Invokes interaction between strangers, new ways of reaching out is also something very important today. The visitor has a contributing function in the artwork. 

What is multidisciplinary about 'Cloud'?

Recycle lamp elements in art. 

Wade Kavanaugh and Stephen B. Nguyen

HUBRIS, ATE, NEMESIS

This massive installation occupies the entire gallery of the Contemporary Art Museum in Maine, inviting users to navigate through the artwork via a path that sometimes bumps into the walls. This adds to the experience of the work as a whole. Named hubris, atë, nemesis, the installation takes its inspiration from Maine's rugged coastal landscape and the tradition of artists who have explored the interplay of natural forces, people and built environment. Its name is derived from Greek tragedies where pride, characterized as an arrogant confidence, turns into atë, a ruinous folly or madness, and ultimately into archenemy, a force of retribution that restores natural order.

What do we find interesting about 'Hubris, Ate, Nemesis'?

The visitor is part of the installation. Can be projected onto everyday life now. Life comes to a standstill, but is therefore filled in again in a very dynamic way.

What is multidisciplinary about 'Hubris, Ate, Nemesis'?

Wood is used in a massive installation. 

Conclusion

HUBRIS, ATE, NEMESIS

From all the projects examined, we have a few preferred projects with which we
want to continue working. These are the CLOUD from Caitlind RC Brown and Wayne Garrett, and HUBRIS, ATE, NEMESIS from Wade Kavanaugh and Stephen B. Nguyen.
In both projects interaction with the spectator is central. The CLOUD urges people to interact with each other through the response the installation evokes. At HUBRIS, ATE, NEMESIS, the focus is on the interaction of the spectator with the installation itself. This interaction of one spectator in turn influences the experience of other spectators.

We want to further investigate how an installation can evoke interaction with and between people, in order to provide an experience that goes further than just viewing an installation.
